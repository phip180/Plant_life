From Molecules to History

Plants do not just support individual bodies. They shape empires, trade routes, and the long arc of civilization.

Timeline

Roots of Civilization

10,000 BCE

The First Harvest. Humans begin domesticating wheat and barley. We stop wandering and start building.

3,000 BCE

Written Word. Papyrus reeds in Egypt allow knowledge to travel across time. History begins.

1600s

Global Exchange. Potatoes and corn cross the ocean, ending famines and fueling population explosions.

Today

Biotechnology. We edit plant genomes to help them survive the changing climate we created.
